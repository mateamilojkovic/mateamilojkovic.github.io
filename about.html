Education

I was homeschooled from the fourth grade until the end of high school. I'm so thankful for this because it allowed me to dig deeper into my studies (like taking Algebra I in 7th grade and dual credit classes my last two years of high school) while exploring extracurricular activities (like acting, food blogging, and tennis). Homeschooling also cultivated in me a curiosity to understand the "why" behind every- and any-thing. I attribute my time management and research skills to my homeschooling as well: my "I might not know but I can find out" attitude was strengthed through my nontraditional education.

I currently attend Winthrop University as a Computer Science and Accounting double major. Fitting these two areas of study into four years while being active in on-campus organizations has definitely had its difficulties. I had to seriously re-evaluate my goals when I spread myself a little too thin in the fall of 2017 when I took 10 courses, was heavily involved in 3 organizations, and worked as a peer tutor. About a month into the semester, I realized I needed to either drop something off my plate or magically make an eight day in the week to catch up on homework. Instead, I took advice I had heard from multiple people I looked up to: plan your day and work your plan. To keep my sanity, I started planning my week to the minute. 5:50AM: wake up. 6:00AM: breakfast. 6:15AM: homework for ACCT 509... and so it went, typically till around 11PM. Thanks to my planner and sheer determination, I was able to continue my participation in extracurricular activities, finish my semester on the Presidents List, and keep my sanity entact.
